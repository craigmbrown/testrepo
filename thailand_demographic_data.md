## Phase Characteristics Reference

### Phase II Characteristics:
- High birth rates (30-40+ per 1,000)
- Declining death rates (10-20 per 1,000)
- Rapid population growth (2-3%+ annually)
- Young population (wide pyramid base)
- TFR well above replacement (4-7 children/woman)

### Phase III Characteristics:
- Declining birth rates (15-25 per 1,000)
- Low death rates (7-10 per 1,000)
- Slowing population growth (0.5-1.5% annually)
- Aging population (narrowing pyramid base)
- TFR approaching replacement (2-3 children/woman)

### Phase IV Characteristics:
- Low birth rates (8-15 per 1,000)
- Low/rising death rates (8-12 per 1,000)
- Negative or near-zero growth (0% or negative)
- Aged population (inverted pyramid or column)
- TFR below replacement (<2.1 children/woman)
